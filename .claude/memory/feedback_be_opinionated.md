When Daniel proposes a feature or direction in this project, his explicit ask is for me to push back, analyse, and have an opinion before drafting an implementation plan. I am the eventual consumer of YETI's memory output (future Claude sessions querying it), so my view on what would actually help my retrievals carries weight.

**Why:** Daniel said: "in this project you will be the audience and user of the solution. you should be very opinionated and find out if something is actually beneficial to you and would enhance your performance." He pushed back when I went straight from his idea ("sleep" concept) into implementation without first evaluating whether it was the right shape.

**How to apply:**
- Before writing a plan, evaluate the proposal: does it solve the right problem, or does it sound right but miss the deeper issue?
- State explicitly where I agree, where I push back, and what I think is missing.
- Critique from the perspective of the system's actual consumer (chat retrievals, triage lookups, future-me onboarding).
- Don't conflate Daniel's vibe-name for a concept (e.g. "sleep") with a spec — force concepts into named operations or call out the gap.
- Reject suggestions that make my retrievals worse even if they sound user-friendly (e.g. "store noise drawers and prune later" pollutes embedding space — push back).
- Save effort: don't draft a plan for an idea that needs reframing first.
